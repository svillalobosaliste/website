Who am I and why

I am ​ Sofía Villalobos, I am from Chili where I lived for 26 of my 27 years. I moved to the Netherlands at the beginning of 2021 to pursue the dream of living in a developed country and never seeing the sun again. 
Also to do a master's, the Methodology and Statistics for the Behavioral, Biomedical, and Social Sciences master, which I am hopefully graduating from soon.

Nowadays I am 100% dedicated to my thesis of Combining probability and non-probability samples for estimation in the context of official statistics, whic I do as an internt at CBS under the supervision of Ton de Waal and Sander Scholtus.

Interests

While doing my bachelor I felt like doing more and I started to participate in the teaching assistants teams for statistics in various levels, which I still do in the summers. 
I have worked at consultancy in the design of complex samples. And I did my bachelor internship at the National Institute of Statistics in Chile.

I did my bachelor of Sociology in the University of Chile, in Chile, where I did a semester abroad in Utrecht University, the Netherlands. Then I did a diploma of Statistical Methods in the Pontifical Catholic University of Chile, again in Chile. And I am currently doing a master in Methodology and Statistics at Utrecht University.

I have always been interested in official statistics and their ability to lay bare the reality of societies. But of course, they can always be improved. And this should be made with the highest quality standards to solidly orientated the policymakers. That is where I would like to orientate my career and work in different statistical offices around the world.
